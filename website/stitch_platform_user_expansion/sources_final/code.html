Data Sources

Incoming Science aggregates content from the following sources. We are grateful to each for making open access to scientific literature possible.

Preprints

arXiv — Cornell University

Daily preprint feeds across physics, mathematics, computer science, quantitative biology, economics, and related fields. arXiv is the primary source for new papers in most fields covered by Incoming Science.

bioRxiv — Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory

Daily preprint feeds across biology and life sciences, including systems biology, bioinformatics, and genetics. bioRxiv papers are routed to the same triage pool as arXiv papers for fields that cover biological topics.

medRxiv — Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory

Daily preprint feeds covering clinical medicine, epidemiology, and health sciences.

NBER Working Papers — National Bureau of Economic Research

Working paper feed covering economics, finance, and public policy research.

CEPR Discussion Papers — Centre for Economic Policy Research

Discussion paper feed covering economics and public policy, with a strong European perspective.

Journal Publishers

American Physical Society (APS)

RSS feeds and full-text abstracts via the APS Harvest API, covering Physical Review Letters, Physical Review B, Physical Review X, PRX Quantum, and Physical Review Applied.

Nature Portfolio

RSS feeds from Nature, Nature Physics, Nature Communications, Nature Photonics, Communications Physics, npj Quantum Materials, npj Quantum Information, and other journals in the Nature family.

AAAS / Science

RSS feeds from Science and Science Advances.

IOP Publishing

RSS feeds from New Journal of Physics, Journal of Neural Engineering, and other IOP journals.

American Chemical Society (ACS)

RSS feeds from Nano Letters, ACS Nano, ACS Photonics, and other ACS journals.

PNAS — National Academy of Sciences

Subject-area RSS feeds from the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences.

Optica Publishing Group

RSS feeds from Optica, Optics Letters, and Optics Express.

SciPost

Open-access RSS feeds from SciPost Physics and its specialised section journals.

The Royal Society

RSS feeds from Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A.

IEEE

RSS and metadata feeds from IEEE Xplore and the IEEE Computer Society, covering IEEE TPAMI, TIP, TSE, TNNLS, and Signal Processing Letters.

ACM

RSS feeds from the ACM Digital Library, covering ACM TOSEM and ACM TIST.

American Economic Association (AEA)

American Economic Review and the American Economic Journals, via Crossref metadata.

Project MUSE — Johns Hopkins University Press

Journal feeds across the humanities, including literature, Jewish studies, and cultural criticism.

University presses

Oxford University Press, Cambridge University Press, University of Chicago Press, and MIT Press — RSS feeds across economics, political science, sociology, and the humanities.

Independent open-access journals

Quantum, JAIR, and Demographic Research — community-run open-access journals with direct RSS feeds.

Additional publishers

Wiley, Springer, SAGE, Taylor & Francis, Cell Press, PLOS, AIP Publishing, EDP Sciences, and Elsevier — providing RSS feeds across social sciences, biology, and interdisciplinary fields.

Abstract Enrichment

For journal articles whose full abstracts are not available in their RSS feeds, Incoming Science queries the following open scholarly databases to retrieve them.

Semantic Scholar — Allen Institute for AI

Batch API used to retrieve abstracts for journal papers not directly accessible via RSS, including ACS journals. Semantic Scholar indexes hundreds of millions of scientific papers across all disciplines.

OpenAlex

Open catalog of scholarly works, authors, and institutions. Serves as the primary article feed for several humanities and social-science journals, and as a fallback abstract source for papers from publishers with restricted access.

Crossref

DOI registration agency metadata API. Used to retrieve article metadata for AEA journals and as a fallback abstract source.

Europe PMC — EMBL-EBI

Open repository of life-science literature. Used as a fallback abstract source for biology and medicine papers.

CORE

Aggregator of open-access research outputs from repositories worldwide. Used as an additional fallback for open-access papers not yet indexed by other sources.
